1.1 What is the Ozone Layer?

  • Located in the stratosphere (about 10–30 miles above Earth)
  • Made of ozone molecules (O₃) = three oxygen atoms
  • Functions like sunscreen for the planet
  • Absorbs UV-B radiation (the harmful ultraviolet light)
  • Protects humans from skin cancer, eye cataracts, immune system damage
  • Protects plants and animals from harmful solar effects
Exam keyword: Ozone = O₃. Don't confuse it with O₂ (the oxygen we breathe).

1.2 How Refrigerants Damage Ozone

The villains: CFCs (chlorofluorocarbons) and HCFCs (hydrochlorofluorocarbons).

  • When released, these rise into the stratosphere and are broken down by sunlight (UV radiation)
  • This releases chlorine atoms (and sometimes bromine)
  • One chlorine atom can destroy 100,000 ozone molecules before it's neutralized
Think of chlorine like a wrecking ball that doesn't stop after one hit.

1.3 Ozone Depletion Potential (ODP)

A scale used to compare how harmful a substance is to ozone.

Baseline = CFC-11 (ODP = 1.0)

CFCs (≈1)
HCFCs (<1)
HFCs/HFOs (0)
CFCs
ODP ≈ 1 (worst)
HCFCs
ODP > 0 but < 1 (harmful but less)
HFCs & HFOs
ODP = 0 (no chlorine, no direct ozone damage)
Exam trap: Don't mix ODP with GWP (Global Warming Potential). They are different environmental metrics.

1.4 Environmental Impact Beyond Ozone

Ozone thinning = "ozone hole" discovered over Antarctica.

☀️
UV Radiation

More UV radiation → skin cancer, cataracts

🌱
Ecosystem Damage

Plankton, crops damaged by increased UV

🌡️
Climate Impact

High GWP refrigerants contribute to global warming

  • Even refrigerants with ODP = 0 can still contribute to climate change through high GWP
  • Example: HFC-134a (no chlorine, ODP = 0, but GWP ~ 1,430)

1.5 Regulations & Protocols

Montreal Protocol (1987)

International treaty to phase out ozone-depleting substances.

U.S. Clean Air Act, Section 608:

  • Illegal to vent refrigerants
  • Requires recovery, recycling, or reclaiming
  • Technicians must be certified
  • HCFCs like R-22 are being phased out completely in the U.S.

1.6 Exam Traps & Memory Hacks

  • Ozone = O₃ → 3 oxygen atoms
  • Baseline ODP = CFC-11
  • CFCs = worst ODP
  • HCFCs = medium ODP
  • HFCs & HFOs = 0 ODP
  • Montreal Protocol = global ozone treaty
  • If you see "chlorine," think ozone damage
"CFC = Chlorine = Catastrophe for ozone."
"HFC = Hydrogen + Fluorine + Carbon = Harmless to ozone."

1.7 Mini-Quiz (Core Practice Style)

Q1.
What does the ozone in the stratosphere consist of?
  • A) O₂
  • B) O₃
  • C) CO₂
  • D) N₂
Answer: B (O₃)
Q2.
Which refrigerants have the highest Ozone Depletion Potential?
  • A) HFOs
  • B) HFCs
  • C) HCFCs
  • D) CFCs
Answer: D (CFCs)
Q3.
What international agreement began the phase-out of CFCs and HCFCs?
Answer: The Montreal Protocol
Q4.
If a refrigerant has ODP = 0, does that mean it has no environmental impact?
Answer: No. It can still have high Global Warming Potential (GWP).
